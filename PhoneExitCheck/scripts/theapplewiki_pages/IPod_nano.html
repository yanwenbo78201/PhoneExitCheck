This article is about the first generation of iPod nano. For the product series, see List of iPod nanos.

The iPod nano was introduced as Apple's replacement for the iPod mini. Through the use of flash storage rather than a mechanical hard drive, the new product line achieves a thinner, smaller, and lighter form factor. Announced by Steve Jobs at the 'Special Music Event' on 7 September 2005 (2005-09-07), it was famously revealed by Jobs taking it out of his jeans watch pocket.

Unlike subsequent iPod nano models, the first generation uses a glossy plastic front plate with chrome back, a design later mirrored in the iPod with video. It is the only iPod nano to use a PortalPlayer processor; its successor is the first iPod to use a Samsung S5L processor. Its operating system is inherited from the iPod photo.

The device is supported by Rockbox.

Battery safety risk

On 11 November 2011 (2011-11-11), Apple issued the iPod nano Replacement Program recall, because "the battery in the iPod nano (1st generation) may overheat and pose a safety risk". It is speculated that the root cause is battery expansion pushing against the click wheel, which is not able to spread the load as other iPods are able to, where the battery is behind the display.[1] The program exchanged any iPod nano (1st generation) with the latest generation of iPod nano available at the time, and ended sometime prior to 19 January 2017 (2017-01-19).[2]

The program is still active in Japan. As of 10 April 2020 (2020-04-10), Apple exchanges the device for a ¥2,000 gift card.
